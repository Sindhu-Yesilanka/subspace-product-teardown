## Overview

This repository contains my product teardown of **Subspace.money**, a platform for subscription sharing, group subscriptions, and discounted digital services.

The analysis focuses on identifying high-impact product, UX, trust, and growth opportunities through direct product exploration, competitor benchmarking, user review analysis, and customer journey evaluation.

## Key Findings

### 1. Trust Is More Important Than Price

Users are primarily concerned about subscription continuity and account access rather than maximizing savings.

### 2. Admin Dependency Creates Risk

Many negative reviews mention:

- Delayed activation
- Non-responsive admins
- Unexpected account removal
- Access interruptions

### 3. Support Visibility Gap

Support exists but users frequently perceive it as difficult to reach, slow, or inconsistent.

### 4. Payment Coordination Friction

Managing recurring subscription payments among multiple users creates social and operational friction.

### 5. Missing Marketplace Guarantees

The platform lacks strong trust signals before payment, increasing perceived risk for new users.

## Conclusion

Subspace has successfully identified a strong consumer need around affordable subscriptions.

The largest opportunity is not further price optimization, but increasing trust, continuity guarantees, support transparency, and reducing payment coordination friction.

These improvements can strengthen activation, retention, and long-term marketplace health.
